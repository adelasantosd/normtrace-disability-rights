# Case-Law Notes
## Disability Rights and Conventionality Analysis in Mexico

This file provides selected case-law notes relevant to disability-rights analysis, conventionality review, and the domestic implementation of international human rights standards in Mexico.

It is intended as a support document for NormTrace. It does not replace full doctrinal case analysis. Its purpose is to identify decisions and interpretive materials that may be relevant when assessing domestic legal compatibility, institutional design, equality, legal capacity, accessibility, and implementation-oriented gaps.

---

## 1. Mexico: Supreme Court of Justice of the Nation (SCJN)

### Contradicción de Tesis 293/2011

**Institution:** Supreme Court of Justice of the Nation (Plenary)  
**Relevance:** Foundational decision for the Mexican constitutional parameter of rights review.

### Analytical significance

This decision is central to the post-2011 rights framework in Mexico. It is commonly cited for the proposition that constitutional and treaty-based human rights standards must be read together within the domestic framework of rights review, subject to express constitutional restrictions where applicable.

### Relevance for NormTrace

Use this decision when:

- identifying the constitutional parameter of rights review in Mexico;
- explaining why domestic legal analysis cannot be limited to constitutional text alone;
- framing the relationship between domestic law and international human rights treaties.

---

### Amparo en Revisión 410/2012

**Institution:** Supreme Court of Justice of the Nation  
**Relevance:** Important for disability analysis and the social model approach.

### Analytical significance

This case is relevant in discussions concerning disability, discrimination, and the move away from restrictive or medicalised understandings of legal capacity and rights.

### Relevance for NormTrace

Use this decision when:

- assessing whether a domestic norm reflects a social or medical model of disability;
- analysing barriers created by legal categories or institutional assumptions;
- connecting disability-rights analysis with constitutional equality principles.

---

### Amparo Directo en Revisión 1387/2012

**Institution:** Supreme Court of Justice of the Nation  
**Relevance:** Relevant to psychosocial disability, guardianship, and legal capacity concerns.

### Analytical significance

This decision may be relevant in contexts where domestic law treats persons with psychosocial disabilities through substituted decision-making or paternalistic legal structures.

### Relevance for NormTrace

Use this decision when:

- analysing legal capacity and supported decision-making issues;
- assessing compatibility with Article 12 CRPD;
- identifying domestic legal provisions that may perpetuate substituted decision-making.

---

### Acción de Inconstitucionalidad 96/2014

**Institution:** Supreme Court of Justice of the Nation  
**Relevance:** Equality and non-discrimination as interpretive principles.

### Analytical significance

This case is useful when situating disability-rights analysis within the broader constitutional framework of equality and non-discrimination.

### Relevance for NormTrace

## 2. Mexico: interpretive and judicial guidance

### SCJN Protocol for adjudicating cases involving persons with disabilities (2014)

**Institution:** Supreme Court of Justice of the Nation  
**Formal status:** Interpretive guidance; not binding precedent.

### Analytical significance

Although it is not binding case law, this protocol is useful because it operationalises a rights-based and disability-sensitive approach to adjudication. It can help identify interpretive expectations relevant to access to justice, accommodation, and non-discrimination.

### Relevance for NormTrace

Use this protocol when:

- analysing access to justice provisions;
- evaluating whether procedural norms account for accommodation;
- identifying interpretive expectations within the Mexican judicial context.

## 3. Inter-American Court of Human Rights

### Almonacid Arellano et al. v. Chile (2006)

**Institution:** Inter-American Court of Human Rights  
**Relevance:** Foundational articulation of conventionality control.

### Analytical significance

This case is a core doctrinal reference for the idea that domestic authorities must assess compatibility between domestic law and the American Convention on Human Rights.

### Relevance for NormTrace

Use this case when:

- explaining the doctrinal basis of conventionality control;
- framing the duty to assess domestic law against international standards;
- introducing the methodology of conventionality analysis.

---

### Trabajadores Cesados del Congreso v. Peru (2006)

**Institution:** Inter-American Court of Human Rights  
**Relevance:** Further development of conventionality doctrine.

### Analytical significance

This case helps show that conventionality control is not confined to a single institutional setting but forms part of a broader duty of rights-compatible domestic adjudication and state action.

### Relevance for NormTrace

Use this case when:

- reinforcing the doctrinal continuity of conventionality control;
- situating domestic review within the Inter-American legal order.

---

### Cabrera García and Montiel Flores v. Mexico (2010)

**Institution:** Inter-American Court of Human Rights  
**Relevance:** Important in the Mexican context for conventionality doctrine.

### Analytical significance

This case is often cited together with the separate opinion of Judge Eduardo Ferrer Mac-Gregor for the systematisation of conventionality control within the Mexican legal conversation.

### Relevance for NormTrace

Use this case when:

- connecting Inter-American doctrine to the Mexican legal context;
- explaining why conventionality analysis is methodologically relevant in Mexico.

---

### Ximenes Lopes v. Brazil (2006)

**Institution:** Inter-American Court of Human Rights  
**Relevance:** Psychosocial disability and standards of care.

### Analytical significance

This case is relevant where domestic law affects persons with psychosocial disabilities, mental health institutions, involuntary treatment, or state duties of protection.

### Relevance for NormTrace

Use this case when:

- analysing mental health legislation;
- assessing institutional settings involving confinement, treatment, or care;
- identifying rights implications for persons with psychosocial disabilities.

---

### Furlan and Family Members v. Argentina (2012)

**Institution:** Inter-American Court of Human Rights  
**Relevance:** Disability and access to justice.

### Analytical significance

This case is especially relevant to disability-sensitive adjudication, procedural fairness, and the need to ensure meaningful access to justice for persons with disabilities.

### Relevance for NormTrace

Use this case when:

- evaluating judicial accessibility;
- analysing procedural accommodation;
- assessing whether domestic law creates barriers to justice.

---

### Gonzales Lluy et al. v. Ecuador (2015)

**Institution:** Inter-American Court of Human Rights  
**Relevance:** Intersectional discrimination.

### Analytical significance

Although not a disability case in the narrow sense, this judgment is important for understanding how multiple axes of disadvantage may interact in rights analysis.

### Relevance for NormTrace

Use this case when:

- analysing intersectional discrimination;
- identifying compounded disadvantage;
- supporting arguments that apparently neutral laws may have differentiated effects across multiple social positions.

## 6. Methodological note for analysts

These case-law notes should be used selectively and analytically.

They are not intended as a checklist of authorities to cite automatically. Their purpose is to help identify:

- doctrinally relevant standards;
- interpretive developments in disability rights and conventionality review;
- constitutional and inter-American principles relevant to domestic legal analysis;
- and possible sources for structured legal argumentation.

In NormTrace, precedents and interpretive materials should be used to clarify the **international standard**, the **domestic legal problem**, and the **type of normative gap** under review.

- **domestic legal framework**

- **international standard**
- **conventionality analysis**
- **normative alignment**
- **legal compatibility**
- **implementation-oriented gap**

These terms should be preferred over ad hoc alternatives unless a specific doctrinal distinction is required.
