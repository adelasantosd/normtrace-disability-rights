# CRPD Key Articles
## Rapid Operational Reference for NormTrace

This file is a rapid operational reference for the **Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD)**. It is intended to support conventionality analysis and international human rights compatibility analysis by identifying key treaty provisions, their minimum standard, common domestic legal red flags, and the main interpretive materials associated with each provision.

This file does **not** replace `references/instruments/cdpd.md`, which remains the fuller substantive reference framework.

---

## Core articles for rapid conventionality and compatibility analysis

| Article | Right / principle | Minimum standard | Typical domestic legal red flag | Key interpretive material |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Art. 3** | General principles | Domestic legal instruments should be interpreted consistently with dignity, autonomy, non-discrimination, participation, accessibility, equality, and respect for difference | Rights language without autonomy, participation, accessibility, or equality logic | CRPD Art. 3 |
| **Art. 4** | General obligations | States must adopt legal and other measures, remove discriminatory laws and practices, and extend relevant duties to public authorities and, where required, private actors | Disability law limited to declarations; no reform pathway for discriminatory norms; no duties for private actors | CRPD Art. 4 |
| **Art. 5** | Equality and non-discrimination | Equal protection and equal benefit of the law; prohibition of disability discrimination; recognition of reasonable accommodation as part of equality duties | Formal equality only; no indirect discrimination analysis; no accommodation duty | CRPD Art. 5; GC No. 6 |
| **Art. 6** | Women with disabilities | Recognition of multiple and intersectional discrimination and adoption of targeted measures | Gender-neutral disability law; no provisions for women or girls with disabilities | GC No. 3 |
| **Art. 7** | Children with disabilities | Best interests, participation, and equal enjoyment of rights by children with disabilities | No child-specific guarantees; no participation logic; adult-centred legal design | CRPD Art. 7 |
| **Art. 9** | Accessibility | Accessibility is a structural, ex ante, cross-cutting duty applying across public life and relevant private settings | Accessibility treated as optional, delayed, or purely programmatic | GC No. 2 |
| **Art. 12** | Equal recognition before the law / legal capacity | Equal legal capacity on an equal basis with others; support in exercising legal capacity; safeguards against abuse | Guardianship, interdiction, substituted decision-making, incapacity presumptions | GC No. 1 |
| **Art. 13** | Access to justice | Equal access to justice with procedural and age-appropriate accommodation | No procedural accommodation; inaccessible courts; no training duties | CRPD Art. 13 |
| **Art. 14** | Liberty and security | Disability must never justify deprivation of liberty | Involuntary institutionalisation or detention based on disability status or diagnosis | CRPD Art. 14; related CRPD interpretive guidance |
| **Art. 19** | Independent living and inclusion in the community | Right to live independently and be included in the community, with support and community-based services | Institutionalisation logic; no support system; family-only care assumptions | GC No. 5 |
| **Art. 24** | Inclusive education | Inclusive education in the general system, with support and reasonable accommodation | Segregated special education as default; no accommodation duty | GC No. 4 |
| **Art. 27** | Work and employment | Equal right to work in open, inclusive, and accessible labour settings; prohibition of discrimination; accommodation duty | No employer duties; no accommodation framework; sheltered work logic replacing inclusion | GC No. 8 |
| **Art. 28** | Adequate standard of living and social protection | Rights-based access to social protection, housing, poverty reduction, and material conditions of life | Purely assistentialist framing; discretionary benefits only; no enforceable access pathway | CRPD Art. 28 |
| **Art. 29** | Participation in political and public life | Equal political rights, including accessible voting and public participation | Capacity-based voting restrictions; inaccessible electoral procedures | CRPD Art. 29 |
| **Art. 33** | National implementation and monitoring | Focal point, coordination mechanism, independent monitoring framework, and civil society participation | Focal point without independence; coordination without authority; no monitoring framework; no meaningful participation of representative organisations | CRPD Art. 33 |

## Rapid analytical prompts for use in NormTrace

For each relevant article, ask:

1. Does the domestic legal instrument recognise the right or principle?
2. If yes, does it create an enforceable legal pathway?
3. Does it identify a competent authority or responsible actor?
4. Does it rely on discretionary or aspirational language instead of binding duty?
5. Does it exclude subgroups or ignore intersectional barriers?
6. Does it shift the burden to the family, charity, private discretion, or future regulation?
7. Does it create a structural, immediate, progressive, or merely symbolic obligation?

## Priority red flags

The following should be treated as priority warning signs in disability-rights analysis:

- substituted decision-making regimes;
- segregated educational structures;
- disability-based deprivation of liberty;
- accessibility treated as optional or indefinite;
- absence of reasonable accommodation duties;
- gender-blind disability legislation;
- family-based care assumptions without state support;
- implementation bodies without independence, authority, participation, or funding.

## Use rule within the repository

This file should be used as a **rapid operational reference** during conventionality analysis or international compatibility analysis.

Where the issue is contested, complex, jurisdiction-specific, or central to the output, the analyst should consult the full file:

- `references/instruments/cdpd.md`

The short file helps identify the relevant article, minimum standard, and likely red flags quickly.  
The full file provides the extended substantive context for deeper analysis.

### Country-use note

This file is instrument-centred rather than country-centred.  
Questions concerning ratification status, Optional Protocol acceptance, domestic incorporation, direct applicability, or institutional implementation should be assessed through the relevant country module.
