# Terminology Guide

This repository uses a controlled set of recurring terms for consistency.

## Core project terms

- **NormTrace**: the project and tool.
- **Normative alignment**: the broader analytical objective of assessing whether domestic legal frameworks adequately reflect applicable international standards.
- **Legal compatibility**: the more specific assessment of whether a domestic legal instrument is compatible with a relevant international legal instrument.
- **Conventionality analysis**: the structured method used to assess compatibility between domestic law and international human rights standards.

## Legal context terms

- **Domestic legal framework**: the relevant set of domestic laws, regulations, and institutional rules in a given jurisdiction.
- **Domestic legal instrument**: a specific law, code, decree, regulation, or other norm.
- **International legal instrument**: a treaty, convention, protocol, or similar formal instrument.
- **International standard**: the applicable norm derived from treaty text and its relevant interpretation.
- **Constitutional parameter of rights review**: the Mexican constitutional and treaty-based framework used for rights review.

## Analytical terms

- **Implementation-oriented gap**: a legal gap linked to institutional design, enforceability, remedies, sanctions, coordination, or operational effect.
- **Institutional anchoring**: the extent to which an international commitment is embedded in domestic legal and institutional structures.
- **Rights-holders**: preferred term for persons protected by legal norms.

## Usage rule

These terms should be used consistently across README files, country modules, analytical frameworks, and case-law notes unless there is a specific doctrinal reason to do otherwise.
