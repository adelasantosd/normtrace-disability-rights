# Terminology Guide

This repository uses a controlled set of recurring terms for consistency across analytical frameworks, country modules, instrument files, case-law notes, and outputs.

These terms should be preferred unless a specific doctrinal distinction requires otherwise.

## 1. Core project terms

### NormTrace
The name of the project and tool.

### Normative alignment
The broader analytical objective of assessing whether a domestic legal framework adequately reflects the applicable international standard.

### Legal compatibility
The more specific assessment of whether a domestic legal instrument is compatible with the relevant international legal instrument.

### Conventionality analysis
The structured method used to assess compatibility between domestic law and applicable international human rights standards.

## 2. Legal context terms

### Domestic legal framework
The broader set of domestic laws, regulations, institutional arrangements, and interpretive structures relevant to the issue under review.

### Domestic legal instrument
A specific law, code, decree, regulation, or other norm under analysis.

### International legal instrument
A treaty, convention, protocol, or similar formal legal source.

### International standard
The applicable norm derived from treaty text together with its relevant authoritative or persuasive interpretation.

### Constitutional parameter of rights review
The constitutional and treaty-based framework used for rights review in the Mexican context.

## 3. Analytical terms

### Implementation-oriented gap
A legal or institutional deficiency linked to enforceability, remedies, sanctions, coordination, accessibility, participation, monitoring, funding, or operational effect.

### Institutional anchoring
The extent to which an international commitment is embedded in domestic legal and institutional structures.

### Rights-holders
Preferred term for the persons protected by a legal norm.

### Structural analysis
The analysis of formal anatomy, institutional design, normative actor mapping, and enforceability pathways of a domestic legal instrument.

### Exclusion analysis
The analysis of direct, indirect, structural, and omission-based forms of exclusion, including gendered, intersectional, indigenous, linguistic, and access-related dimensions.

### Coding-ready summary
A structured output format intended to support later comparative, quantitative, or mixed-method analysis without replacing interpretive legal judgment.

## 4. Terms to avoid using interchangeably without reason

The repository should avoid uncontrolled alternation between the following:

- `domestic legal framework` / `national law` / `municipal law`
- `international standard` / `international obligation` / `global norm`
- `legal compatibility` / `compliance`
- `rights-holders` / `beneficiaries`
- `block of constitutionality` / `constitutional parameter of rights review`

These pairs are not always equivalent and should not be treated as if they were interchangeable.

## 5. Preferred usage rules

- Use **domestic legal framework** when referring to the broader legal and institutional setting.
- Use **domestic legal instrument** when referring to the specific norm under review.
- Use **international legal instrument** for treaties and similar formal sources.
- Use **international standard** for the applicable rule derived from text and interpretation.
- Use **normative alignment** for the broad analytical objective.
- Use **legal compatibility** for the specific contrast between domestic and international law.
- Use **implementation-oriented gap** for deficiencies linked to operational legal effect.
- Use **rights-holders** unless critically describing the language of a domestic legal instrument.

## 6. Methodological note

Terminological consistency is part of methodological rigour in NormTrace.

The purpose of this guide is not stylistic uniformity for its own sake, but conceptual clarity. Consistent terminology makes it easier to:

- compare outputs;
- support future coding;
- avoid category drift;
- and preserve analytical coherence across jurisdictions and domains.
